1. The first time we met

Lost in Translation

A beautiful film about feeling lost within a new world. Obviously, we didn’t know what was to come between us but I found myself falling in love with this film the way I fell in love with you. The way the two meet and slowly build up to the relationship is something I love in a movie. And definitely something I’d like to associate with the first time we met. It kind of feels like dominoes falling in place. We simply just met in class yet everything after was slow, confusing and sometimes tiring, like this film lol. It was a “slow-burn” if you wanted to put a name to it.

2. Our group project

Booksmart

Booksmart was such a funny and interesting film. To be honest with you, I kind of only chose this movie because of the title but it is genuinely a great watch. I’m pretty sure you’ve watched this but it’s basically like the gen z version of Superbad. This was the first group project I had in my very first university class and I was very much terrified. In some way, I relate to the two main characters, I wanted to show that I was something more than just work. I also very much relate to the character Amy but for different reasons…

5. The Confession

Okay SO. Let me preface that this is not a very great comparison. In fact, this is a red flag comparison. However, I don’t know why but during this time, I was reminded of this film. 7. Just Kidding

Just kidding!!

8. The Wollongong Trip

So, this doesn’t really fit in with this section but I remember that I had just watched this film before going on our trip. A dark and ‘black mirror’-esque film that follows the pursuit of love. It’s gory, emotional and made me never want to use a dating app or do whatever they were doing. I loved the cinematography, it was very blue and stale which I felt fit well with the film’s context.

8. and more...

Howl's Moving Castle

The Wollongong drive was very peaceful, much like all of Miyazaki’s films. I specifically chose Howl’s Moving Castle as there was such beautiful scenery throughout the whole film. Maybe I'm looking back at that day with foggy lenses but it was really pretty. I really enjoy exploring places with you. Just sitting and watching everything made that day one I’ll never forget.

9. Settling in

Shirkers

A slightly different type of movie genre ~ Shirkers is a dreamy documentary about longing and searching for meaning within the past. The mood of this documentary is whimsical and deeply intriguing. When I think of comfort films, I think of the tale this movie tells. I believe it’s still on Netflix, I highly recommend it!!

11. The Quiet Days

In Mood for Love

The quiet days are what we make of them. I’d come over to your house or vice versa and we’d just eat and hang out, watching movies and doing other stuff. I associate quiet days with quiet movies. Something that leaves things up to interpretation. In The Mood For Love is an ode to quietness. The dark streets of Hong Kong, the background chatter of people, the gestures of longing. Truly one of my favourite movies of all time!
